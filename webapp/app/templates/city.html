Air Quality

Air quality is measured with the air quality index, or AQI, a standard maintained by the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency. It takes into consideration five major air pollutants: ground-level ozone, particle pollution, carbon monoxide, sulfur dioxide, and nitrogen dioxide. AQI values span from 0 to 500, with 0 being clean and 500 being extremely hazardous.
