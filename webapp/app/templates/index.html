Seeing Air
An experiment in citizen-run, DIY air quality monitoring

Those of us who live in more open societies can take for granted the freely available information we have access to. In many countries it is difficult to get reliable information on something as basic as air quality. [1]

As sensors become cheaper and more abundant, a future in which this type of data is more freely accessible becomes possible. As part of a partnership between Swissnex, Grey Area and Lift Conference, more than 100 batteries of air quality sensors were set up in seven cities across the globe. These collect readings every 10 seconds, and are freely accessible to anyone with an internet connection. [2]

This project is an exploration of how this type of data could be used to increase understanding of air quality. Each city has its own panel of visualizations. These can answer questions that affect the quality of life: Is the air ever hazardous? If so, how often? What is the air like during commute hours?
